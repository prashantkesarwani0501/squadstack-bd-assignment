2. The Crisis: Regulatory Tsunami Hitting Revenue

üö® Critical Business Challenge

Q2 2025 vs Q2 2024: 40% drop in brokerage revenues

In August 2025, founder Nithin Kamath publicly stated: "The time has finally come for business to pivot." This isn't typical startup speak‚Äîthis is Zerodha admitting they're in crisis mode.

What's Causing the Revenue Collapse?

1. STT Increase on F&O Trading (Oct 2024)

Securities Transaction Tax on options trading increased significantly. Since F&O trading generates most of Zerodha's brokerage revenue, this directly hit their bottom line.

2. Elimination of Exchange Transaction Rebates

Exchanges used to pay brokers rebates for high trading volumes. This was 10% of Zerodha's revenue. Now completely eliminated by regulators.

3. Reduction of Weekly Options Expiries

Limited to two weekly contracts, reducing trading opportunities and thus trading volume (which means less brokerage revenue).

4. BSDA Limit Increase

Basic Services Demat Account limit increased, making more accounts eligible for reduced/free services.

5. ASBA for Trading

New Application Supported by Blocked Amount requirements adding operational complexity.
